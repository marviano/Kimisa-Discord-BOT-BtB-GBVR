Overview

Vikala is a space control nuisance that employs RNG items to secure space and whittle down the opponent. In addition to tossing items manually akin to traditional item characters, Vikala can eject them from her Dream Attraction cannonsGuardAllStartup18Recovery30Advantage-4 directly at the opponent.

When items can't cut it, Dormouse and friends can harass the opponent as long-range strikesGuardAllStartup15RecoveryTotal 52Advantage+2 and bear trapsGuardAllStartup32+6RecoveryTotal 44Advantage that give enough advantage on hit to cycle between each other, or to set up items.

While hurling the entire circus at the opponent can be fun, Vikala has a few areas of struggle. For starters, her defense is among the worst in the game, with a floaty jump, a 6f c.L, no meterless reversal, and a subpar ultimate reversal. Further, her offense is very lacking and she will mostly rely on universal strings and setplay gimmicks to open opponents up.

If you can handle an eccentric, perhaps cheesy battle style and don't mind picking up after being dealt unlucky items, Vikala is sure to give her fans a gouda time.

Cons
  • Strong Space Control: Vikala has a large assortment of attacks and traps that cover many areas of the screen, and 214UGuardAllStartup13Recovery20Advantage-4 and 236UGuardAllStartup16RecoveryTotal 57Advantage+8 can both blast through the opponent's zoning. In addition, f.H and j.H cover fair range in front of Vikala.
  • Midscreen Setplay: Marching TeethGuardAllStartup32+6RecoveryTotal 44Advantage can restrict the opponent's movement, be plus as a meaty, or otherwise aid Vikala in her mixups with Triple Attack and her Overhead dash attack.
  • RNCheesus Giveth: Due to the random nature of Vikala's items, both the player and the opponent must constantly adapt to shifting neutral conditions if she's leading the pace of the match. Assailing BombsGuardAllStartupL+72RecoveryAdvantage, Spike BallsGuardAllStartupRecoveryAdvantage+12 and StarsGuardAllStartupRecoveryAdvantage-6 can be specially hard to navigate for the opponent. In pressure and combos, a lucky item appearing on the item tracker can grant her situational extensions.
  • Poor Defense: Vikala does not have a meterless reversal, and her fastest normal is 6f. Her Ultimate Reversal has shallow range and counts as a projectile, and can therefore be nullified by certain attacks such as Headbutt of LoveGuardUnblockableStartup22Recovery34AdvantageHKD +38.
  • Awkward Anti-Airs: Vikala's 2H is quite vertically small and she lacks a conventional DP without spending meter, sometimes relying on the finnicky Rodent Rhythm to cover some angles.
  • Blind Mice: Most of Vikala's specials can either be circumvented by 6G or are simply too minus to be pressure tools unless resources are spent. As such, Vikala heavily relies on her deployable attacks to threaten the opponent.
  • Floaty Jump: Vikala takes longer to land from a jump with virtually no upside, making her particularly vulnerable to being counter attacked when jumping.
  • RNCheesus Taketh: Most of Vikala's items offer no direct impact on the match besides being glorified projectiles. Though she can track what items come out next, they shift every 5 seconds, making situations post long animations such as a throw vary greatly in advantage.


Unique Mechanic: Items

Vikala's Items are randomly occurring and can be deployed into the match through either version of Dream Come True or any version of Dream Attraction. There is no Item lockout, and as such multiple may be active at a time.

All 8 Item effects can be seen here

Despite their disorderly nature, the next Item is always previewed through an Item Tracker labeled "NEXT" that sits below Vikala's UI. Items are switched out within the Tracker every 300 frames5 seconds, blinking before they rotate away. This timer ticks regardless of what Vikala is doing, even during a Throw animation. Knowing this, caution must be exercised and the player must display semi-constant knowledge of what the Tracker currently displays.
